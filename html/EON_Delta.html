Conflict in the Niger Delta

Poverty, corruption and the presence of oil, arms and gangs, have made the Niger Delta a very volatile region. In the past years, armed groups and criminal gangs have explicitly sought to control resources, and have engaged in acts of violence. This has lead to an increase in violent confrontations between the armed groups and the Joint Task Force (JTF). The JTF is composed of troops of the army, navy, air force and the mobile police, and was set up in 2004 to restore order in the Niger Delta.

The security forces, including the military, continue to commit human rights violations in the Niger Delta, including extrajudicial executions, torture or other ill-treatment, and destruction of homes. In the run-up to the 2007 elections, violence in the Delta increased as politicians used armed gangs to attack their opponents. After the elections, the situation deteriorated further. Armed groups and gangs kidnapped dozens of oil workers and their relatives, including children, and attacked many oil installations.

The JTF has been frequently accused of using excessive force when attacking armed groups and gangs and often bystanders from local communities were injured and killed. In August 2008, following an attack on the JTF by armed groups, at least 4 people were killed when the military raided the village of Agge, Bayelsea State. The JTF attacks on the communities in the area are continuing, reportedly because they believe the armed groups are hiding in the communities.

In August 2007, the JTF intervened in a clash between two rival gangs in Port Harcourt, Rivers State, using helicopters and machine-guns and killing at least 32 gang members, members of the security forces and bystanders.

In May 2009, 20,000 people who live in Warri South and southwest local government area, in the Niger Delta region of Nigeria, were caught in cross fire between the Joint Task Force (JTF) and armed groups. Thousands have since then fled their communities and were unable to return to their homes.

The JTF offensive began on 13 May 2009 after the JTF was reported to have been attacked by armed groups in Delta State. The JTF have been conducting land and air strikes on communities across the Warri south and south-west local government areas where the Nigerian government believes the camps of the armed groups are located. Hundreds of people were killed.

The JTF attacked several communities of the Gbaramatu Kingdom, including Okerenkoko and Oporoza, using helicopters equipped with machine guns on 15 May 2009. Around 500 people had gathered in Oporoza for a yearly festival that was being celebrated in several communities of the Gbaramatu Kingdom.
