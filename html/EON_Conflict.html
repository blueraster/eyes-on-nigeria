Communal Conflict in Nigeria

In the city of Jos, Plateau state, where thousands of people have been killed in repeated incidents of violence in 2001, 2004, 2008 and 2010, several inquiries have been established but their findings and recommendations have never been made public. Victims have not received redress or reparation, or compensation. Criminal investigation has been inadequate, with serious doubts over the quality of evidence against persons arrested. No one has been held accountable.

As recently as December 2010, dozens of people were killed in gun and machete attacks in and around Jos, during an outbreak of communal violence. The violence followed the detonation of at least three bombs in Jos on 24 December 2010, which killed over 80 people. Dozens of houses were burnt down and businesses remain shut.

Hundreds of people have been killed in sectarian violence in and around Maiduguri, Borno state, since July 2009, by members of the religious sect formally known as Boko Haram. More than 140 people, including members of Nigeria's security forces, have been killed since January in bombings by Boko Haram in northern Nigeria. The group has taken responsibility for a number of attacks specifically targeting police and government officials, religious leaders and churches, and bars and beer gardens, killing scores of civilians. Suspected members of Boko Haram also carried out attacks in Bauchi and Yobe states, killing at least five policemen. In response to abuses by Boko Haram, Nigerian security forces have unlawfully killed dozens of people, and many have been brutally assaulted and unlawfully detained.
